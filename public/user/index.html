One session. Three steps.

PhotoCuller is designed around a single review session. Open a folder or ingest a card, make your decisions, route the output, move on.

1

Ingest

Plug in an SD card and PhotoCuller can detect it automatically. Choose a destination template, apply an optional IPTC preset, and start copying. Files appear in the grid in real time so you can begin reviewing before ingest finishes.

2

Cull

Grid view is built for speed. Use WASD to navigate, number keys to rate, F to flag, R to reject, and filters to narrow quickly. Loupe helps check sharpness without leaving the grid.

3

Deliver

Build workflows with filters for rating, flags, color labels, stacks, and file type, then route to folders, NAS, or Apple Photos with template-driven naming. Enable multiple workflows and execute them in one run.

Questions & Answers

Everything you need to know about PhotoCuller

How is PhotoCuller different from Lightroom?

PhotoCuller is focused purely on culling - quickly reviewing, rating, and organizing photos before editing. It's a one-time purchase with no subscription, runs completely offline, and integrates seamlessly with Apple Photos. Think of it as the fastest way to get from thousands of shots to your best selects.

Does it work with Apple Photos?

Yes! You can load albums from your Apple Photos library, cull your shots with ratings and color labels, then route your picks back to Photos or to external drives.

Can I send photos to external editors?

Yes. Add your preferred external editors in Settings, then open files directly from PhotoCuller. For RAW+JPEG pairs, you can choose whether to open the RAW file or the JPEG version before handing off to your editor.

What file formats are supported?

PhotoCuller supports RAW files from most major camera brands (Canon, Nikon, Sony, Fujifilm, and more), plus JPEG, HEIC, PNG, and TIFF. It automatically detects and pairs RAW+JPEG files shot together.

Can I use it completely offline?

Absolutely. PhotoCuller runs entirely on your Mac with no internet connection required. Your photos never leave your device - there's no cloud upload, no account needed, and no tracking.
